Vector-Borne Diseases and Climate Change

Rising temperatures and shifting rainfall patterns are intensifying the spread of mosquito-borne diseases such as malaria, dengue, and Zika. Researchers highlight how climate change is reshaping vector distribution across Africa, Asia, and even parts of Europe and North America. Modern predictive tools like GIS and remote sensing are now being used to anticipate outbreaks and strengthen public health responses.

Mental Health Impacts of Climate Change

Beyond physical health, climate change is affecting mental well-being. Extreme heat in 2024 led to a nearly 10% rise in psychiatric hospital admissions in the UK. Flood risks and unpredictable weather events are increasing stress and anxiety among communities. Researchers emphasize the need for integrated climate and mental health policies.
