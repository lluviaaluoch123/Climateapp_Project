How ClimateDigest Has Helped

Community Awareness

“Through ClimateDigest, we learned about sustainable farming practices. The platform has given us knowledge to adapt to changing weather patterns. For instance, adopting drought-resistant crops like sorghum and utilizing simple techniques like ziz-zag trenches has enabled us to secure at least one reliable harvest each year, drastically reducing our dependence on erratic rainfall.”

Youth Engagement

“ClimateDigest has empowered young people in our community to take action. We now organize clean‑up drives and tree planting campaigns. The platform gave us the organizational tools and educational resources to start the 'Green Generation' club, which has mobilized over 150 volunteers to plant 5,000 seedlings along the local riverbanks.”

Policy Influence

“By sharing our stories on ClimateDigest, local leaders have started listening. Our voices are being heard in climate policy discussions. After submitting photographic evidence of local wetland destruction, our County Assembly passed a motion to enforce stricter environmental impact assessments, demonstrating that grassroots digital advocacy works.”

Community Resilience

“ClimateDigest connected us with other communities facing similar challenges. Together, we share solutions and build resilience.We used the peer-to-peer mapping tool to find coastal communities in Kwale successfully implementing mangrove restoration projects, allowing us to replicate their successful low-cost method to protect our own eroding shorelines.”

Voices from the Ground

Hear directly from people across Kenya, Africa, and beyond about how climate change is affecting their lives.

Amina – Turkana County, Kenya

“Our livestock have been dying due to prolonged droughts. Water sources have dried up, and families are forced to walk long distances for survival. Climate change has made our traditional way of life uncertain.”

Kwame – Accra, Ghana

“Heavy rains and flooding have destroyed homes in my neighborhood. We rebuild every year, but the floods keep coming stronger. Climate change is no longer a distant issue—it’s here with us.”

Lydia – Nairobi, Kenya

“Unpredictable weather has made farming difficult. Crops fail more often, and food prices rise. Climate change is affecting our ability to feed our families.”

Samuel – Cape Town, South Africa

“Water shortages have become a constant worry. Climate change has made us rethink how we use and conserve water in our city.”
